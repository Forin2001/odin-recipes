FUFU DISH

Description

Fufu is a starchy, doughy West African dish made from boiled and pounded root vegetables,
such as cassava, yams, or plantains. The word "fufu" comes from the Twi language of Ghana and Ivory Coast, and means "mash" or "mix"

INGREDIENTS
  • boiled cassava
  • plantains
  • cocoyam
STEPS
  1. Peel and chop: Peel and finely chop the cassava and plantains
  2. Blend: Blend the cassava and plantains with water until smooth.
  3. Cook: Transfer the puree to a pot and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  4. Shape: Once the fufu has thickened and changed color from white to yellow, poke holes in the top and add water to cover about 75% of the surface. Cover the pot, reduce heat, and cook for at least five minutes.
  5. Serve: Serve the fufu in a wet bowl and let it cool for a few minutes. With wet hands, shape the fufu into a ball and serve with your choice of soup.
